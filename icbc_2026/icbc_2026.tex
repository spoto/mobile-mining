\begin{abstract}
  This paper advocates the use of mobile phones for blockchain mining.
  Namely, they are a large and cheap hardware base, which is
  interesting, in particular, to start up a new blockchain, when it is
  hard to convince people to install complex software
  on a continously connected computer. This paper acknowledges that mobile phones
  have hardware limitations that make most mining algorithms impractical,
  with the notable exception of proof of space. The latter preallocates
  a large data structure on disk, later used for inexpensive mining,
  in terms of CPU cost and exchanged data: this
  copes with the limited resources of a mobile phone.
  This paper presents our new Android app Mokaminter for mining blockchains based
  on the generic Mokamint proof of space engine.
  It confirms its low battery usage,
  reduced exchanged data and ability to work
  in adverse network conditions. To the best of our knowlegde,
  Mokaminter is the first mobile app for mining.
\end{abstract}
